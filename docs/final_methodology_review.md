# ILONAA Final Methodology Review

## Executive Summary

ILONAA is a privacy-first decision-support tool that helps people interpret AI-related career pressure. It combines profession-level occupational structure, a RIASEC identity layer, and four user calibration answers to produce one primary user-facing outcome: AI Replacement Risk.

Methodology communication was updated to prioritize trust, emotional relevance, and clarity while keeping the underlying model unchanged.

## Why We Chose A Single User-Facing Score

- **Reduced cognitive load:** users no longer need to decide which score matters.
- **Clearer user experience:** one dominant result answers one dominant question.
- **Stronger positioning:** ILONAA clearly owns the replacement-risk interpretation space.
- **Better trust:** less dashboard clutter and fewer conflicting signals.
- **Easier communication:** media, HR, recruiters, and investors can describe the product quickly.
- **Easier marketing:** one headline outcome simplifies messaging across channels.
- **Easier sharing:** users can remember and share one primary result with context.

## Profession Selection

- Users search and select the closest profession title.
- Matching is based on exact/prefix/contains and term-overlap ranking.
- Chosen profession establishes baseline factors and baseline scores before personalization.
- If an exact role does not exist, users select closest professional equivalent.

## O*NET Integration

Imported/derived occupation fields:

- Identity fields: `code`, `title`, `alternateTitles`, `description`
- RIASEC fields: full six-type score map + primary/secondary types
- Occupational descriptors: work activities, styles, skills, abilities, context
- Factor layer:
  - routine/repetitive
  - information processing
  - data analysis
  - administrative structure
  - human interaction
  - creativity/innovation
  - decision judgment
  - consequence responsibility
  - physical practicality
  - adaptability/learning
- Baselines:
  - baseline AI exposure
  - baseline career resilience
  - baseline risk index

## RIASEC Integration

- RIASEC is used as occupational identity/context.
- Primary + secondary types are shown in user results.
- RIASEC is not treated as a deterministic replacement-risk engine.
- It explains role orientation (e.g., Social vs Investigative), not destiny.

## Four ILONAA Questions (exact wording)

1. **How often do you use AI tools in your work today?**
2. **How quickly do you learn new digital tools?**
3. **How much of your work depends on uniquely human strengths?**
4. **What could happen if a critical decision in your role goes wrong?**

## AI Risk Model (conceptual, non-formula)

What factors matter and why:

- **Routine structure:** more repeatable tasks can increase exposure.
- **Current AI capability proximity:** high AI-enabled workflow contact can increase transition pressure.
- **Human judgment requirement:** higher contextual judgment generally increases replacement resistance.
- **Accountability and consequence:** legal, safety, and financial responsibility reduce full replacement likelihood.
- **Human relationship depth:** trust-intensive roles tend to resist full automation.
- **Physical presence/hands-on work:** real-world execution often reduces replacement likelihood.
- **Adaptability:** faster adaptation improves resilience over time.

How factors influence outputs:

- **AI Replacement Risk (primary):** reflects whether core accountable responsibility can realistically be substituted.
- **AI Exposure (supporting):** internal dimension affected by task profile and AI-capable workflow characteristics.
- **Career Resilience (supporting):** internal dimension reflecting protective human factors and adaptation capacity.

Positioning language retained:

- AI Replacement Risk remains explicit.
- "Risk is not destiny."
- "Exposure does not automatically imply replacement."

## Examples Included in Public Methodology

1. **Financial Controller**
   - High accountability and financial consequence with moderate AI exposure.
   - Interpretation: reporting/analysis can be automated in part, but decision ownership remains strongly human.
2. **Teacher**
   - High human interaction, trust, and relationship depth.
   - Interpretation: AI supports prep/admin; teaching remains socially contextual and human-centered.
3. **Data Scientist**
   - High AI exposure with high adaptability and technical expertise.
   - Interpretation: rapid transformation with high resilience potential for adaptive practitioners.

## Legal Review

### Endorsement risk: MEDIUM

- Mitigation: explicit statements that ILONAA is not endorsed by O*NET, U.S. Department of Labor, universities, or John Holland.
- Residual risk: ensure all public copy consistently uses "inspired by" / "aligned with" language.

### Trademark risk: LOW-MEDIUM

- O*NET and Holland references are descriptive and contextual.
- Mitigation: avoid brand-styled implication of affiliation.

### Unsupported claims risk: LOW-MEDIUM

- Public copy avoids outcome guarantees.
- Continue avoiding deterministic employment statements.

### Scientific overclaim risk: MEDIUM

- Mitigation: keep claims at "structured interpretation" level.
- Avoid wording that implies validated predictive certainty without formal validation publication.
